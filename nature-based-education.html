Nature-Based Education

Why the forest is the best classroom for curious young minds.

Our Approach

Learning That Grows Naturally

At Foothills Forest School, we believe the outdoors is the most powerful classroom available. Our nature-based approach is rooted in decades of research showing that children who spend meaningful time in nature develop stronger cognitive abilities, emotional resilience, and social skills.

We align our educational objectives with children's natural curiosity about outdoor exploration. Rather than bringing children indoors to learn about the world, we take them into it.

Our Philosophy

Three Pillars of Learning

🌲 Pillar One: Outdoor Education

Spending time in natural environments promotes a remarkable balance of development across the whole child:

  • Cognitive growth through exploration and problem-solving
  • Emotional resilience through navigating real challenges
  • Social skills through cooperative outdoor play
  • Motor coordination and spatial awareness
  • Fine and gross motor skill development
  • Early scientific thinking through observation of natural phenomena

👩‍🏫 Pillar Two: Our Team

Our educators are not just qualified — they are passionate advocates for outdoor learning. Every member of our staff approaches teaching with the belief that:

  • Each child is a unique individual deserving individual attention
  • Learning should never be rushed
  • Children are capable of far more than we typically expect
  • The role of the teacher is to guide, not dictate
  • Outdoor environments inspire natural curiosity and learning

🎨 Pillar Three: Play-Based Learning

Play is not a break from learning — it is learning. Our play-based curriculum incorporates:

  • Child-led nature discovery and exploration
  • Climbing and balancing for physical development
  • Environmental stewardship and care for the natural world
  • Multiple learning modalities to reach every learner
  • Self-awareness and social-emotional development
  • Understanding each child's global impact

The Research

Why Nature-Based Education Matters

A growing body of research confirms what forest school educators have known for generations: children learn best when they are active, engaged, and connected to the natural world. Studies consistently show that nature-based learning leads to:

Academic Benefits

Improved concentration, creativity, and academic engagement when compared to traditional indoor settings.

Physical Health

Reduced rates of childhood obesity, improved gross motor development, and stronger immune systems.

Mental Wellbeing

Lower anxiety, fewer behavioral challenges, and stronger emotional regulation in children who spend regular time outdoors.

Environmental Stewardship

Children who learn in nature develop a lifelong connection to the environment and commitment to caring for the world.
