Experience Nature Based Learning

Pillar One

Outdoor Education

Through a holistic approach, outdoor education promotes a balance of cognitive development, emotional resilience, and malleable social skills.

Our time in nature provides an exquisite opportunity to develop a young child's bilateral movement and coordination, proprioceptive balance, spatial awareness, gross and fine motor skills...the list is truly endless.

Beyond the physical advantages, outdoor play significantly contributes to cognitive growth.

Pillar Two

Our Team

The team at Foothills Forest School consists of qualified educators who are committed to providing quality education outdoors. Their expertise ensures that each child receives personalized attention and guidance.

Our staff represents a diverse web of backgrounds coming together here to build something that weaves different modalities and meaningful learning into an outdoor immersive classroom. We deeply value the natural environment that serves as a dynamic classroom where children can explore and make sense of the world around them. We are committed to seeing each child for their own individual full self and honoring what it means to lean into learning with them at their pace.

Pillar Three

Play Based Learning

Our unique program at Foothills Forest School incorporate play-based learning and environmental stewardship. Children engage in activities that build skills and promote a lifelong love for the outdoors.

Young children engaging in outdoor play are given the opportunity to seamlessly improve motor skills, agility, and coordination. Activities like running, climbing, and balancing on uneven terrains help build muscle strength and appropriately engage their risk assessment capabilities. For instance, observing plants, insects, and weather patterns fosters curiosity and supports early scientific thinking. This type of open-ended exploration is crucial for stimulating young minds and promoting problem solving skills.
